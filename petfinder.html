PetFinder Mobile App

PetFinder is a mobile application designed to find lost dogs, cats, parrots, hamsters, iguanas, spiders, and our other little friends that we love so much.

It was created as a part of my portfolio with a view to show an easy, fast, and effective way of using just one app to search for lost pets instead of making posters and posts on various social networks.

My roles

  • Product Strategy
  • User Research
  • Visual Design
  • Prototyping & Testing

Background and understanding the problem

Every time we pass a cat or a dog sitting alone on the street, we wonder if they are homeless or just like to walk and play freely. What if we could find out in seconds with a phone camera and a mobile app?

Product vision and solution

There are no similar apps in Russia, so pet owners still use various social network groups for searching lost pets, which takes a lot of time and not that effective.

I decided that solution here is a simple, nice, and easy to use product for both the owners and for those who help with finding pets, as they are an important link in the chain of a successful reunion.

How it works

You don’t have to create an account to start using the app!

See a cat or a dog sitting somewhere without a leash or an owner? Check if this animal is missing in four steps!

  1. Open the PetFinder
  2. Choose camera mode
  3. Take a picture
  4. Press “Check”

User Persona Groups:

Olivia — Lost her dog

Olivia is a 35 year old female living with a partner in St. Petersburg, Russia, and she lost her dog Sam.

Olivia is an economist and has been working remotely these past few months, usually taking Sam for a walk 3 times a day near her home.

“Yesterday evening, Sam got scared of the fireworks and ran away while we were walking. I can’t find him and I need help.”

Olivia’s goals, needs, and motivation:
"I want to find my dog."

Elena — Owns a cat

Elena is a 22 year old single female living with her cat, Mimi, in St. Petersburg, Russia.

She is a student and has been studying online these past few months, so she likes to take Mimi for a walk five times a day near her home.

“Mimi is a member of my small family and I love her so much!”

Elena’s goals and needs:
“I want to help other dog and cat owners find their missing pets”.

Elena’s motivation:
she puts herself in the place of pet searchers and empathizes with them.
